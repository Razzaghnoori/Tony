 December 6 1994 Name Address City ST Zip Dear Name As 1994 draws to a close we at the School of Nursing pause to reflect on all we have accomplished in the 80 years since our founding in 1914 At this time we also turn to friends like you asking you to show that you share in our sense of pride by continuing to support the School s Annual Fund In a recent message to current students I urged them too to take pride in being associated with one of the best nursing schools in the country and reminded them of our rich heritage our role in founding Sigma Theta Tau International and in launching the Midwest Nursing Research Society and Midwest Alliance in Nursing our leadership in bringing the Healthy Cities movement to the United States and designation as the first and only World Health Organization Collaborating Center in Healthy Cities our ranking among the top 16 schools of nursing in the 1994 U S News and World Report survey and our support for the numerous students alumni and faculty who have won awards and held leadership positions in community university and professional organizations This past academic year highlights have included the establishment of a Board of Visitors the appointment of two new campus deans Marian Pettengill at IU South Bend and Penny Cass at IU Kokomo recognition of the Community Health Nursing Department by the American Public Health Association sponsoring two nurse recipients for IU honorary degrees Hildegard Peplau at IUPUI and Elizabeth Carnegie at IU Southeast and celebrating the induction in 1993 and 1994 of four more faculty into the American Academy of Nursing We also take great pride in the innovative teaching of our award winning faculty who have developed the use of information technology in learning situations and earned recognition at Founder s Day ceremonies for the past three years and in the everyday heroism of our students exemplified by the winners of our Profiles in Courage awards an inoculation program organized by BSN students at IUPUI and the contributions of IU Southeast students to St John s Day Center Reminding ourselves of our excellence isn t done to be egotistical Rather it allows us to feel energized and be pleased that we chose to be a part of a school that has achieved national recognition Gifts to the School s Annual Fund provide critical dollars for scholarships student activities and faculty development areas not covered by tuition or state dollars Regardless of the size of your gift your participation in the annual drive is an indicator of your pride in association with the School of Nursing If your gift has already been mailed to us please accept our sincere thanks for your generosity If not I hope you will send in your renewal gift today Sincerely Angela Barron McBride Distinguished Professor and University Dean
